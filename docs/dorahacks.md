Introduction
StableHacks 2026 is an online, global hackathon for startups and experienced developers building institutional-grade stablecoin infrastructure on Solana.

The hackathon targets real-world, scalable use cases at the intersection of DeFi and TradFi, with a focus on regulatory-aligned, security-first, production-ready solutions.

StableHacks includes four focus tracks. Please choose one track and submit your build under that theme. The examples provided are meant to serve as guidance and are not intended to restrict other possibilities. We encourage innovative solutions beyond the listed examples if they align with the core track. Solutions aligned with our partners’ focus areas are especially welcome and may increase potential for real-world pilot opportunities.

Prerequisites
All tracks and proposed solutions must be able to operate in the context of regulated institutions under control of financial market regulators. Please consider following components as mandatory elements for the solution for every track.

KYC (Know Your Customer) is the process financial institutions use to verify the identity of a client, confirm they are legitimate, and assess the risk they may pose. It is required to prevent financial crime, fraud, money laundering, and sanctions violations.

KYT (Know Your Transaction) is the process of monitoring and analysing transactions to detect suspicious, illegal, or high‑risk activity in real time.

AML (Anti‑Money Laundering) refers to the laws, controls, and processes that financial institutions must follow to detect, prevent, and report money laundering and terrorism financing.

The Travel Rule is an AML (Anti‑Money Laundering) requirement that forces crypto service providers (VASPs such as exchanges, custodians, and banks) to collect, verify, and share information about the sender and receiver when transferring crypto assets above a certain threshold. Its purpose is to prevent money laundering, terrorism financing, and other illicit activity.

About us
